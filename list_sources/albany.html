Ph.D. and M.S. DEGREE PROGRAMS IN THE BASIC SCIENCES

Students are afforded the opportunity to join in the excitement of discovery, to engage in the challenges of research and contribute to the scientific community.  Entering students quickly become part of research teams in one of our basic science departments and accept the challenge of improving the health of the public by scientific advancement. Upon completion of the program, students become independent biomedical scientists equipped with the scientific and technologic expertise in their chosen field of specialization.

 

BASIC SCIENCE DEPARTMENTS

     


General information about Ph.D. and M.S. degrees is provided here. For more detailed information on specific requirements for each of the Basic Science Departments please view the Department sites. 

DOCTOR OF PHILOSOPHY DEGREE IN THE BASIC SCIENCES

Ph.D. Time to Degree

The course of study for the Ph.D. takes approximately five to six years when important milestones are met in a timely fashion.The thesis must be defended within seven years from the date of initial registration, except under extenuating circumstances. 

Ph.D. Course Work and Research

A minimum of 74 credit hours is required for the Ph.D. degree. The number of required course work credits is between 24 and 30, depending on the Department and 50 credit hours of research.The required credit hours of course work is comprised of didactic and elective courses selected by the student in consultation with a mentor.The required number of credits does not include credits for journal clubs. A course in Ethics is also required.All students are required to demonstrate competence in English in the oral and written communication of their research.Upon recommendation of a Department or Thesis Committee, students may be required to undertake remedial English training.

In the event that a student's GPA falls below the acceptable level of 3.0, the student may request transfer to the M.S. program. After completion of the requirements for the M.S. program, including thesis defense, a student may reapply to the Ph.D. Program.

 

Ph.D. Examinations

Doctoral students are required to pass three examinations: The Qualifying Examination, the Candidacy Examination, and the Thesis Defense.  In order to take these examinations, students must be in good academic standing as defined by a minimum GPA of 3.0 and satisfactory grades in research.

All components of the Qualifying Examination must be completed by the end of September at the start of the third year of fulltime enrollment.  By the end of September at the beginning of the fourth year, doctoral students must pass the Candidacy Examination.  Students' progress toward meeting these deadlines will be monitored by the Graduate Student Progress Committee.

QUALIFYING EXAMINATION
Both the format and scope of the exam are specified by the Department.  Each Department will define the passing criteria prior to the exam.  The purpose of the exam is to evaluate student progress in two areas: 1)  comprehensive evaluation of the student's scientific background and 2)  evaluation of the student's ability to use that knowledge to address research problems. If the student fails the Qualifying Exam, he/she will have to retake the exam within six months of the original exam but not later than December of the third year.

CANDIDACY EXAMINATION
The Candidacy Committee consists of the mentor (nonvoting) and five voting members.  The committee is selected by the graduate student, mentor, and Department Director.  This examination is based upon a NIH-style research proposal submitted by the student to his/her Candidacy Committee at least two weeks prior to the exam.  The research proposal must follow the standard NIH guidelines and be based upon the student's proposed area of research.  In the event that a student fails the Candidacy Examination, he/she will have to retake the exam within six months of the original exam but no later than the end of March of the fourth year.  The Candidacy Examination is not open to the public.

THESIS DEFENSE COMMITTEE
The Thesis Defense Committee is required to meet at intervals of six months to review the progress of the thesis research and to make appropriate recommendations.

FINAL DATA SET MEETING
At the time of the final Data Set meeting, the doctoral student must have at least one peer-reviewed journal article (accepted for publication or published), on which the student is the first author.  At this meeting the thesis committee will need to approve the complete data set and final statistical analyses of the data that will comprise the thesis.

DEFENSE OF THESIS FOR THE DOCTOR OF PHILOSOPHY
Copies of the thesis must be submitted to the members of the Thesis Defense Committee at least three weeks before the scheduled date of the defense.  A minimum of four voting Committee members must be present at the defense.  The candidate will present a public seminar of the thesis research after which the Thesis Defense Committee will evaluate the thesis in closed session.  The student will be expected to defend his/her thesis in terms of hypothesis, experimental approach, data interpretation, conclusions, and future studies.  A defense is successful if a student receives at least four PASS votes.  If the thesis defense is not successful on the initial attempt, a second defense may be granted upon the recommendation of a majority of the Thesis Defense Committee and the recommendation of the Associate Dean for Graduate Studies, subject to approval by the Executive Committee.  The thesis must be defended within seven years from the date of initial registration, except under extenuating circumstances.

Predoctoral Research Assistantships

Predoctoral Research Assistantships are $28,000 (pre-tax) and are normally provided to all qualified basic science doctoral students during their first three years of study. After the third year of study, student research support must be provided from extramural funds, most notably NIH research or NIH training grants. Financial support provided by Research Assistantships and Research Fellowships are available to qualified doctoral students and are awarded competitively.  All students normally receive either an Assistantship or a Fellowship throughout the time period of their Ph.D. studies. Additional financial assistance may be arranged through the Department to which the student has applied and through the Albany Medical College Office of Financial Aid (518) 262-5435 or FinAid@mail.amc.edu.

MASTER OF SCIENCE DEGREE IN THE BASIC SCIENCES

M.Sc. Time to Degree

Full time M.S. students are required to complete all requirements and receive their degree within two to three years - except in extenuating circumstances.

M.Sc. Course Work and Research

A minimum of 32 credit hours is required for the M.S. degree. The number of required course work credits is between 12 and 18, depending on the Department and 20 credit hours of research.  Course work is comprised of required and elective courses selected by the student in consultation with a mentor. The required number of credits does not include credits for journal clubs. A course in Ethics is also required. All students are required to demonstrate competence in the oral and written communication of their research in English. Upon recommendation of a Department or Thesis Committee, students may be required to undertake remedial English training. 
 

M.Sc. Examinations

In order to qualify for the thesis defense, students must be in good academic standing, with a minimum G.P.A. of 2.5.  

Students in the M.S. Degree program will select a thesis committee by September 1 of their second year of study.The Thesis Committee consists of the mentor who is the nonvoting chair and three voting members. 

The M.S. Thesis Committee will meet every six months to monitor the student's progress and to fine-tune their research program.At least two months prior to the defense of the Master's thesis, the student must convene a thesis committee meeting for the presentation of the data sets that comprise the thesis.At least two of the three voting members of the student's thesis committee must agree that the data sets are sufficient for the thesis and that no more experiments are necessary. A final target date for the thesis defense will be scheduled at this meeting.The thesis defense will be conducted as described previously for the Ph.D. program except that the public seminar is optional. The evaluation of the M.S. Thesis Defense is similar to that of the Ph.D. Defense.At least two out of three voting committee members must cast a PASS vote for the thesis defense to be successful.

Employment of Basic Science M.Sc. Students during their period of Study

The Master of Science degree programs at Albany Medical College require students to immerse themselves in an intense and integrated program of courses and research. For these reasons, outside employment is strongly discouraged. Students are encouraged to visit the Albany Medical College Financial Aid Office for other possible solutions.

Prospective students should also note (as stated below in this webpage) that for full-time Masters students the cost of the annual tuition ($26,804) is fully covered by Trustee Scholarships!  Thus Masters students primary costs are living expenses and health insurance.


 


COMPLETION AND CONFERRAL OF M.S. AND Ph.D. DEGREES
 

Upon completion of the Basic Science degree requirements, students must submit a PDF version of their thesis to the Graduate Studies Office and to ProQuest, an information-content company where copies of all Albany Medical College graduate theses are maintained. 

The instructions for writing and submitting your thesis to ProQuest are available by CLICKING HERE. Please read all instructions and discuss them with your mentor before logging into the site. The ProQuest Theses Subject Categories List, which is referred to in the instructions, is available by CLICKING HERE. 

You may also buy bound copies of your thesis from ProQuest. 

Additionally, both MS and PhD students should go to the Graduate Studies Office where they will be given a Basic Science Graduate Studies Program Clearance/Exit Form. Students will be awarded their degree on their transcript and have their diploma sent to them within 30 days of submission of this completed form.

 

M.D.-Ph.D. PROGRAM

A combined program of study which leads to the awarding of both an M.D. and Ph.D. degree is available to students who wish to pursue a career in research and academic medicine.  Such programs usually take seven or eight years to complete both degrees.  Students generally complete the first two years of medical school, followed by three or four years of graduate study and thesis preparation, and then return to complete their final two years of medical school in ambulatory and hospital-based clinical clerkships.  Stipends and tuition waivers are available during the years when the student is enrolled as a PhD candidate. 

Application to this program is generally made during the freshman or sophomore year of medical school.  Students are not directly recruited into the M.D./Ph.D  program.  Additional information is available by contacting the Graduate Studies Program Office at (518) 262-5253.
